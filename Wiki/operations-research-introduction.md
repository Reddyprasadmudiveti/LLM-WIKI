## Meaning of Operations Research
Operations Research (OR) is a scientific approach to decision-making that involves the application of analytical methods to help make better decisions (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg). It employs techniques from mathematical modeling, statistical analysis, and mathematical optimization to find optimal or near-optimal solutions to complex problems (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg). Essentially, it's about optimizing operations and resources.

## Scope of Operations Research
The scope of OR is vast and applies to numerous fields where resources are limited and need to be allocated efficiently (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg). Common areas include:
- **Manufacturing and Production**: Inventory control, production planning, supply chain management (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
- **Finance**: Portfolio optimization, risk management, capital budgeting (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
- **Transportation and Logistics**: Routing, scheduling, fleet management (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
- **Healthcare**: Hospital resource allocation, staff scheduling, patient flow (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
- **Marketing**: Marketing mix optimization, sales forecasting (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).

OR provides quantitative tools, such as [[linear-programming-problem]], to support managerial decision-making (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
