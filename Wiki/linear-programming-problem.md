## Definition of General LPP
Linear Programming (LP) is a mathematical modeling technique used to determine the best possible outcome (such as maximum profit or lowest cost) in a given mathematical model whose requirements are represented by linear relationships (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg). 

A general Linear Programming Problem (LPP) consists of:
1.  **Decision Variables**: The variables that the decision-maker can control (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
2.  **Objective Function**: A linear function of the decision variables that needs to be maximized or minimized (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
3.  **Constraints**: Linear inequalities or equations that represent the limitations or restrictions on the resources (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
4.  **Non-negativity Restrictions**: Decision variables cannot be negative (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).

## Formulation of LPP
The process of formulating an LPP involves translating a real-world problem into a mathematical model (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg). The steps generally include:
1.  **Identify the Decision Variables**: Determine what needs to be calculated or decided (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
2.  **Identify the Objective**: Determine whether the goal is to maximize or minimize. Formulate the objective function mathematically (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
3.  **Identify the Constraints**: Determine the limitations (budget, materials, labor hours). Express these as linear inequalities or equations involving the decision variables (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).
4.  **State the Non-negativity Constraint**: Ensure all decision variables are explicitly stated to be greater than or equal to zero (source: operations research - Sunitha.jpg).

Once formulated, an LPP can be solved using techniques like the [[lpp-graphical-method]] or the [[simplex-algorithm]].
